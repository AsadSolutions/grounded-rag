# Client Confidentiality Policy

**Policy number:** POL-2210
**Applies to:** All personnel, contract staff, and vendors with access to client information
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Office of the General Counsel

## Purpose

Acme Legal Partners LLP holds client confidences as a foundational professional obligation. This policy sets out how client information is classified, how conflicts of interest are checked before a matter is accepted, how information barriers are established, and how a suspected breach of confidentiality must be reported.

## Data classification

All client-related information handled by the firm falls into one of four classifications:

- **Public:** information the client has authorized for public release.
- **Internal:** administrative information not tied to specific client matters, such as firm-wide scheduling.
- **Confidential:** matter-specific information, including case strategy, financial terms, and correspondence.
- **Privileged:** information protected by attorney-client privilege or work-product doctrine.

Confidential and Privileged materials may never be transmitted over unencrypted channels and may not be stored on personal devices. Staff handling Privileged materials must use firm-issued, encrypted devices as described in the Remote Work Policy, POL-2250.

## Conflict checks

Before any new matter is accepted, the responsible partner must submit a conflict check request to the Conflicts Committee. The Conflicts Committee clears or flags the request within 2 business days of submission. No engagement letter under POL-2201 may be issued until the conflict check has cleared. If a conflict is identified but the affected clients consent in writing, the matter may proceed under an information barrier, described below.

## Information barriers

When a conflict is waived by informed client consent, the firm establishes an information barrier, sometimes called an ethical wall, around the matter. Attorneys and staff on one side of the wall may not discuss matter details, access shared files, or attend strategy meetings related to the matter on the other side of the wall. The Conflicts Committee documents every information barrier in a written memorandum, circulated to all personnel named in the wall, and reviews each active wall quarterly to confirm it remains necessary.

## Non-disclosure agreements

Every contract staff member, temporary paralegal, and third-party vendor who will have access to Confidential or Privileged client data must sign a non-disclosure agreement before receiving system access. NDAs are administered by Human Resources and filed with the Records Department. Vendors providing services such as document review, e-discovery, or court reporting are contractually bound to the same confidentiality standards as firm employees.

## Reporting a suspected breach

Any employee, partner, or contractor who suspects that client information has been disclosed without authorization must report the matter to the General Counsel's office within 24 hours of discovery. Reports may be made by phone or in writing. The General Counsel's office logs every report, regardless of severity, and determines within 3 business days whether client notification or regulatory reporting is required. Retaliation against an employee who reports a suspected breach in good faith is strictly prohibited.

## Client communications

Attorneys must confirm a client's preferred and approved communication channel before sending Confidential or Privileged material. Where a client has requested that certain family members, co-parties, or third parties be excluded from communications, that restriction is noted in the matter file and must be honored by every timekeeper on the matter.

## Departing personnel

When an attorney or staff member leaves the firm, IT disables system access on the departure date, and the responsible partner for each of that person's active matters confirms in writing that no client files were retained on personal devices or personal cloud storage. Exit confirmations are filed with the Records Department alongside the personnel file.
