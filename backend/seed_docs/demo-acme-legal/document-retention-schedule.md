# Document Retention Schedule

**Policy number:** POL-2230
**Applies to:** Records Department, all timekeepers, and IT
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Office of the General Counsel, administered by the Records Department

## Purpose

This schedule defines how long Acme Legal Partners LLP retains client and firm records before destruction, and the process for suspending destruction when a litigation hold is in effect. Consistent retention protects client interests, satisfies regulatory obligations, and limits unnecessary long-term storage risk.

## Retention periods by record type

- **Client matter files:** retained 7 years after the matter is formally closed in the timekeeping system, then eligible for destruction.
- **Trust account (IOLTA) records:** retained 10 years from the date of the last transaction on the account, reflecting the firm's fiduciary duty over client funds.
- **Corporate formation records:** retained permanently. This includes articles of incorporation, partnership agreements, and other foundational documents the firm prepares for corporate clients.
- **Billing records and engagement letters:** retained 7 years from the invoice date, aligned with the client matter file retention period under this schedule and referenced in the Engagement and Billing Policy, POL-2201.
- **Personnel records:** retained 7 years after an employee's departure date, administered by Human Resources.

## Matter closure

A matter is considered closed when the responsible partner marks it closed in the timekeeping system and confirms that all outstanding invoices have been resolved. The 7-year retention clock for that matter's client file begins on the closure date, not the date the matter was opened. Matters that remain open for more than 24 months without billing activity are flagged by Finance for partner review to confirm whether the matter should be formally closed.

## Litigation holds

When the firm reasonably anticipates litigation or receives formal notice of a claim involving a matter, the General Counsel's office issues a litigation hold. A litigation hold immediately suspends the standard retention schedule for every record related to the affected matter, regardless of how close the file is to its scheduled destruction date. Records under a litigation hold may not be destroyed, altered, or transferred outside the firm's custody. A litigation hold remains in effect until the General Counsel's office lifts it in writing; only the General Counsel's office has authority to lift a hold.

## Destruction process

Once a record's retention period expires and no litigation hold applies, the Records Department schedules it for destruction. Physical files are shredded by a bonded vendor under a confidentiality agreement consistent with POL-2210. Electronic records are destroyed using secure deletion methods that prevent recovery. For every destroyed file, whether physical or electronic, the Records Department completes a certificate of destruction, recording the matter number, the record type, the retention period applied, and the date of destruction. Certificates of destruction are themselves retained permanently as evidence of compliance.

## Client requests for early return of files

A client may request the return of their original documents before the standard retention period expires. Such requests are handled by the responsible partner, who confirms with the General Counsel's office that no litigation hold applies before releasing original materials. The firm retains a copy of any returned file for the remainder of the standard 7-year retention period unless the client's engagement letter specifies otherwise.

## Backup and disaster recovery copies

Backup copies of electronic records, including those created for disaster recovery purposes, follow the same retention and destruction rules as the primary record. Backup systems are configured to purge expired records on the same cycle as the Records Department's destruction schedule, reviewed quarterly by IT in coordination with the Records Department.
