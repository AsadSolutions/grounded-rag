# Leave and Benefits Policy

**Policy number:** POL-2240
**Applies to:** All full-time employees of Acme Legal Partners LLP
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Human Resources

## Purpose

This policy describes paid time off accrual, sick leave, parental leave, paid holidays, and retirement benefits for full-time employees at both the Meridian City and Port Callahan offices. Part-time and contract staff are not eligible for the benefits described in this policy unless otherwise stated in their engagement terms.

## Paid time off accrual

Paid time off, referred to as PTO, accrues based on tenure:

- Employees with under 3 years of tenure accrue 15 days of PTO per year.
- Employees with 3 or more years of tenure accrue 20 days of PTO per year.

PTO accrues monthly, in equal increments, starting from an employee's hire date. Newly hired employees begin accruing PTO immediately but may not use PTO during their first 90 days of employment except in the case of a documented medical need.

## PTO carryover and cash-out

Unused PTO may be carried over into the following calendar year up to a maximum of 5 days. Any PTO beyond the 5-day carryover cap is forfeited at year-end unless the employee elects to cash it out. Employees may cash out up to 5 days of unused PTO per year at their current base pay rate, subject to Finance approval submitted before December 15.

## Sick leave

Employees accrue 10 days of sick leave per year, credited at the start of each calendar year. Sick leave does not roll over between years and unused sick leave is not eligible for cash-out. Sick leave may be used for the employee's own illness or to care for an immediate family member. Absences beyond 3 consecutive sick days require a note from a healthcare provider, submitted to Human Resources.

## Parental leave

Employees who have completed at least 6 months of employment are eligible for 12 weeks of paid parental leave following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. Parental leave may be taken continuously or, with Human Resources approval, split into two blocks within the first 12 months following the qualifying event. Parental leave runs concurrently with any leave required under applicable law and does not draw down an employee's separate PTO or sick leave balances.

## Paid holidays

Acme Legal Partners LLP observes 10 paid holidays each year, published annually by Human Resources before January 1. Employees required to work on a published holiday due to a client deadline are eligible for a substitute day off, to be taken within 60 days and approved by their supervising partner.

## Retirement benefits

The firm sponsors a 401(k) retirement plan. Acme Legal Partners LLP matches employee contributions up to 4% of base salary. Matching contributions vest immediately; there is no vesting schedule delay. Employees are eligible to enroll in the 401(k) plan starting on their first day of employment, with the first payroll deduction beginning the following pay cycle.

## Leave of absence

Employees requiring an extended unpaid leave of absence beyond what is covered by PTO, sick leave, or parental leave must submit a written request to Human Resources at least 30 days in advance where the need is foreseeable. Human Resources responds to leave of absence requests within 10 business days.

## Requesting time off

PTO and sick leave requests are submitted through the firm's HR system and require approval from the employee's supervising partner. Requests for PTO of 5 or more consecutive days should be submitted at least 15 business days in advance to allow for matter coverage planning, consistent with client service obligations described in the Engagement and Billing Policy, POL-2201.

Questions about PTO balances, leave requests, or benefits enrollment should be directed to Human Resources.
