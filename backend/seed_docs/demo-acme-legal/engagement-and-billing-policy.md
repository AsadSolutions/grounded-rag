# Engagement and Billing Policy

**Policy number:** POL-2201
**Applies to:** All timekeepers at Acme Legal Partners LLP
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Finance Department, in coordination with the Managing Partner's office

## Purpose

This policy governs how Acme Legal Partners LLP opens new client matters, sets billing rates, records time, and issues invoices. It applies to every attorney, paralegal, and contract timekeeper across both offices, in Meridian City and Port Callahan. Any deviation from this policy requires written approval from the Managing Partner.

## Engagement letters

No billable work may begin on a client matter until a signed engagement letter is on file with the Records Department. The responsible partner must issue the engagement letter within 5 business days of the matter being opened in the timekeeping system. Engagement letters must state the scope of representation, the applicable hourly rates, and the estimated fee range where practicable. Matters opened without a signed engagement letter within the 5-day window are flagged by Finance for partner review and may be suspended from further billing until the letter is executed.

## Standard hourly rates

Acme Legal Partners LLP bills on a standard rate card, reviewed annually each January by the Managing Partner's office:

- Partner: $650 per hour
- Senior Associate: $475 per hour
- Associate: $410 per hour
- Paralegal: $185 per hour

Rates may be discounted for volume clients only with written approval from the Managing Partner, documented in the engagement letter. Discounts are never applied retroactively to time already recorded.

## Time recording

All timekeepers record time in six-minute increments, equivalent to 0.1 of an hour. Time entries must be entered into the timekeeping system within 3 business days of the work being performed. Entries that are vague (for example, "review file") are returned by Finance for revision before an invoice can be generated. Block billing across unrelated tasks in a single entry is discouraged; timekeepers should separate distinct tasks that individually exceed 0.5 hours.

## Invoicing and payment terms

Invoices are generated monthly, on the first business day of each month, covering time recorded in the prior calendar month. Standard payment terms are net 30 days from the invoice date. Clients who have not paid within 30 days of the invoice date are assessed late payment interest of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance, applied on the 31st day past due and compounding monthly thereafter. Finance sends a courtesy reminder at 15 days past due before interest begins to accrue.

## Retainers

New matters for clients without an established payment history require an initial retainer of $5,000, collected before work begins. The retainer is held in the firm's trust account and drawn down as invoices are issued. When the retainer balance falls below $1,000, Finance automatically issues a replenishment request to the client for an additional $5,000. Matters may not continue active billable work if the retainer balance reaches $0 and a replenishment request has gone unanswered for more than 10 business days.

## Write-offs and fee adjustments

Any write-off of billed or unbilled time exceeding $2,500 on a single matter requires prior written approval from the Managing Partner. Write-offs under $2,500 may be approved by the responsible partner alone but must be recorded with a written reason in the timekeeping system. Fee disputes raised by a client are routed to the Managing Partner's office for review within 10 business days of the client's written objection.

## Billing records and retention

Copies of all invoices, time detail, and engagement letters are retained by the Records Department for 7 years from the date of the invoice, consistent with the retention period for client matter files described in the Document Retention Schedule, POL-2230. Trust account records related to retainers are retained separately for 10 years under the same policy.

Questions about this policy should be directed to the Finance Department or the Managing Partner's office. Requests for rate exceptions must be submitted in writing and will be tracked against the client's engagement letter file.
