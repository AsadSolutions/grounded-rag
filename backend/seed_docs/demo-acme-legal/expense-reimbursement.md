# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Policy number:** POL-2260
**Applies to:** All employees and partners of Acme Legal Partners LLP
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Finance Department

## Purpose

This policy governs how employees submit and are reimbursed for business expenses, including client-billable expenses, travel, and the home office equipment stipend described in the Remote Work Policy, POL-2250.

## Submission window

Expense reports must be submitted within 45 days of the date the expense was incurred. Expenses submitted after 45 days are not eligible for reimbursement except with written approval from the Managing Partner, granted only in extraordinary circumstances such as extended medical leave.

## Receipts

A receipt is required for any single expense over $25. Expenses of $25 or less may be submitted without a receipt but must still include a written description of the business purpose. Missing receipts for expenses over $25 will cause the line item to be rejected by Finance until a receipt or written justification is provided.

## Mileage

Employees using a personal vehicle for business travel are reimbursed at a rate of $0.62 per mile. Mileage must be logged with the starting location, destination, and business purpose of the trip. Ordinary commuting between an employee's home and their assigned firm office is not reimbursable mileage.

## Meals while traveling

Meal expenses while traveling for business are reimbursed up to $75 per day, covering breakfast, lunch, and dinner combined. Alcohol is not reimbursable under this cap and must be itemized separately if included on a shared receipt. Client meals are governed separately and should reference the client matter number so the expense can be billed appropriately under the Engagement and Billing Policy, POL-2201.

## Approval thresholds

Expense reports totaling $1,000 or less may be approved by the employee's supervising partner. Any single expense report totaling more than $1,000 requires managing partner sign-off before reimbursement is processed. Expenses related to client travel that will be billed to the client should note the matter number so Finance can route the charge correctly on the client's next invoice.

## Reimbursement processing

Once an expense report is approved, Finance processes reimbursement within 15 business days. Reimbursements are paid via direct deposit using the same bank account as payroll. Employees can check the status of a submitted expense report at any time through the Finance system.

## Equipment stipend claims

The $500 annual home office equipment stipend described in POL-2250 is claimed through the standard expense process. Stipend claims should be tagged as "Home Office Equipment" and do not require managing partner sign-off regardless of amount, since the $500 annual cap is enforced automatically by the Finance system.

## Non-reimbursable expenses

The following are never reimbursable under this policy: parking or traffic violations, personal entertainment unrelated to client development, expenses for a spouse or guest without prior Managing Partner approval, and any expense that would violate the Client Confidentiality Policy, POL-2210, such as printing costs at an unsecured third-party print shop for Confidential materials.

## Client-billable expenses

Expenses incurred directly on behalf of a client, such as court filing fees, expert witness costs, or travel required for a specific matter, are recorded separately from personal reimbursable expenses. Client-billable expenses must reference the matter number and are passed through to the client's invoice under the terms of the applicable engagement letter, without markup unless the engagement letter specifies otherwise.

## Questions

Questions about expense reports, reimbursement status, or the equipment stipend should be directed to the Finance Department.
