# Remote Work Policy

**Policy number:** POL-2250
**Applies to:** All non-partner employees of Acme Legal Partners LLP
**Effective date:** March 1, 2022
**Owner:** Human Resources, in coordination with IT Security

## Purpose

This policy sets the standard hybrid work arrangement for Acme Legal Partners LLP, the security requirements for working outside a firm office, and the equipment support available to remote employees. Partners are not subject to the in-office day requirement but must still comply with the security requirements below whenever accessing firm systems remotely.

## Hybrid work requirement

Non-partner employees are required to work a minimum of 3 days per week from a firm office, either Meridian City or Port Callahan. The remaining days may be worked remotely, subject to supervising partner approval and client coverage needs. Supervising partners set specific in-office days for their teams to support collaboration on active matters; employees should confirm their team's schedule with their supervising partner.

## Core collaboration hours

Regardless of remote or in-office status, all employees are expected to be available during core collaboration hours of 10:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. local time, Monday through Friday. Meetings involving multiple timekeepers should be scheduled within core hours whenever possible. Employees working outside standard business hours to meet a client deadline should record that time consistent with the Engagement and Billing Policy, POL-2201.

## Equipment and stipend

The firm provides a home office equipment stipend of $500 per year to employees who regularly work remotely. The stipend may be used for items such as a monitor, chair, or docking station and is reimbursed through the standard expense process described in the Expense Reimbursement Policy, POL-2260. All primary work must be performed on a firm-issued laptop; personal computers may not be used to access firm systems or client data.

## Security requirements

Every remote connection to firm systems must go through the firm-managed VPN. Split-tunneling or other configurations that route firm traffic outside the VPN are prohibited. Firm-issued laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled and multi-factor authentication configured on all accounts with access to Confidential or Privileged client information, consistent with the Client Confidentiality Policy, POL-2210. IT Security conducts a quarterly compliance check of remote devices; devices found out of compliance are remotely locked until the issue is resolved.

## Working from outside the country

Employees who wish to work remotely from outside the country for more than 5 consecutive business days must submit a request to Human Resources at least 10 business days in advance. Requests are reviewed jointly by Human Resources and IT Security to confirm that data residency, export control, and client confidentiality obligations can still be met from the proposed location. Some jurisdictions are excluded from approval due to legal or security constraints; the current exclusion list is maintained by IT Security and available on request.

## Shared and public workspaces

Employees working from a shared or public workspace, such as a co-working space or coffee shop, must use a privacy screen when viewing Confidential or Privileged material and must not conduct client calls where the conversation could reasonably be overheard. Printing client documents in a shared workspace is prohibited.

## Office access for remote employees

Remote employees retain full badge access to both firm offices and are encouraged to reserve a desk in advance through the firm's scheduling system when planning an in-office day, particularly during weeks with high office occupancy.

## Non-compliance

Repeated failure to meet the 3-day in-office minimum without supervising partner approval, or a security compliance failure identified during a quarterly device check, may result in suspension of remote work privileges, reviewed by Human Resources on a case-by-case basis.

Questions about hybrid scheduling should go to your supervising partner. Questions about VPN, encryption, or device compliance should go to IT Security.
