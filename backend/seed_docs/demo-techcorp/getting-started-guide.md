# Getting Started with Atlas

**Product:** Atlas by TechCorp, Inc.
**Applies to version:** v4.2
**Document reference:** DOC-3002
**Owner:** Product Team

## Before you begin

This guide walks through creating an Atlas workspace, inviting your team, and completing the first-run checklist. It applies to Atlas version v4.2 and assumes you do not yet have an account. If you already have a workspace, see the Product Overview for a description of plans and roles instead.

## Creating an account

Go to the Atlas signup page and enter a work email address. Atlas sends a verification link that expires after 24 hours; if it expires, request a new one from the signup page. Once your email is verified, you will be asked to set a password.

## Password requirements

Atlas passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include at least 1 number and 1 symbol. Passwords are checked against a list of commonly leaked passwords at creation time; if a match is found, you will be asked to choose a different password. This same password policy applies to every Atlas account regardless of plan, and is described again in the Security FAQ for reference.

## Starting a free trial

New workspaces begin on a 14-day free trial of the Team plan, with no credit card required to start. During the trial you have access to all Team plan features, including Reports and Integrations, for up to 25 seats. At the end of the 14-day trial, you will be prompted to choose a paid plan; workspaces that do not select a plan are downgraded to a read-only state rather than deleted, and can be reactivated at any time by adding billing information.

## Creating your workspace

After choosing a password, you will be prompted to name your workspace and select a data residency region if you are starting on a Business or Enterprise trial. Workspace names can be changed later from workspace settings by an Admin or the Owner.

## Inviting your team

From the workspace sidebar, select "Invite teammates" and enter email addresses, one per line. Each invited teammate receives an email invitation valid for 7 days. You can assign a role, Admin, Member, or Viewer, to each invitee at the time of the invite, or leave the default of Member. Invitations that expire unused can be resent from the "Pending invitations" list.

## Connecting your first integration

Atlas supports integrations with common chat, calendar, and storage tools. From workspace settings, select "Integrations" and choose a tool to connect. Most integrations use an OAuth flow and take under a minute to authorize. Custom integrations built against the Atlas API are described in the API Basics guide.

## Creating your first Board

From the workspace home screen, select "New Board" and choose a layout: list, kanban, or calendar. Boards can be switched between layouts at any time without losing data. Add your first few tasks directly from the Board, or import tasks from a CSV file using the import option in the Board menu.

## First-run checklist

New workspaces are shown a first-run checklist covering four steps: create a workspace, invite at least one teammate, connect an integration, and create a first Board. Completing all four steps unlocks the Atlas onboarding badge, visible on your workspace profile, and is purely cosmetic.

## Keyboard shortcuts

Atlas supports keyboard shortcuts for common actions: press "C" to create a new task from anywhere in a Board, press "/" to open search, and press "G" then "B" to jump to your Boards list. A full shortcut reference is available from the help menu in the bottom-right corner of the app.

## Getting help

If you run into an unexpected error while getting started, consult the Troubleshooting Guide, which lists common error codes and their fixes. For questions not covered by documentation, use the in-app support chat, which routes to the support tier associated with your plan as described in the Product Overview.
