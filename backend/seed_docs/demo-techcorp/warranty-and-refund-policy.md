# Warranty and Refund Policy

**Product:** Atlas by TechCorp, Inc.
**Document reference:** DOC-3301
**Version:** 2.1
**Owner:** Legal and Billing Team

## Purpose

This policy describes the refund windows available for Atlas subscriptions, the cancellation terms for Enterprise contracts, the uptime service level agreement, and what is and is not covered by Atlas's limited warranty.

## Refund windows for monthly and annual plans

Customers on a monthly billing cycle may request a full refund within 14 days of their most recent charge. Customers on an annual billing cycle are covered by a 30-day money-back guarantee from the date of the annual charge. Refund requests after these windows are evaluated on a case-by-case basis by the Billing Team but are not guaranteed.

## Enterprise contract cancellation

Enterprise contracts require 60 days of written notice to cancel, submitted to the customer's account representative or through the support portal. Enterprise customers who cancel after 90 days of active service on their current contract term receive a prorated refund for the unused portion of the term, calculated from the effective cancellation date. Enterprise customers who cancel within the first 90 days of a contract term are not eligible for a prorated refund but may still be eligible under the standard 30-day annual refund window if applicable.

## Uptime service level agreement

TechCorp commits to 99.9% monthly uptime for the Atlas platform, measured across all core modules: Boards, Workflows, Reports, and Integrations. Scheduled maintenance windows, announced at least 48 hours in advance, are excluded from the uptime calculation. If monthly uptime falls below the 99.9% commitment, affected customers receive a service credit:

- Uptime below 99.9% but at or above 99.0%: a 10% service credit applied to the following month's invoice.
- Uptime below 99.0%: a 25% service credit applied to the following month's invoice.

Service credits are the sole remedy for uptime shortfalls and are not issued as cash refunds. Credits must be requested within 30 days of the end of the affected month.

## Limited warranty

TechCorp warrants that Atlas will perform substantially in accordance with its published documentation when used as intended on a supported browser. This warranty does not cover issues caused by unsupported browsers, third-party integration failures outside TechCorp's control, or modifications made through unofficial or unauthorized API usage. Supported browsers are the current and prior major release of each of the leading evergreen browsers; Atlas is not guaranteed to function correctly on browsers outside this support window.

## What is not covered

The following are explicitly excluded from both the limited warranty and the uptime service level agreement: outages caused by a customer's own network or ISP, data loss resulting from a customer disabling their own backups or export settings, and performance issues caused by exceeding documented API rate limits described in the API Basics guide.

## Data export on cancellation

Customers who cancel their subscription, on any plan, retain the ability to export their workspace data in a standard format for 30 days following cancellation. After 30 days, workspace data is permanently deleted in accordance with TechCorp's data retention practices, and cannot be recovered.

## Disputing a charge

Customers who believe they were charged in error should contact the Billing Team through the support portal before initiating a chargeback with their payment provider. Chargebacks initiated without first contacting the Billing Team may result in temporary suspension of the workspace pending resolution.

## How to request a refund or credit

Refund and service credit requests are submitted through the support portal under the "Billing" category. Standard-tier requests are processed within the same response window as general support, described in the Troubleshooting Guide. Enterprise requests are handled directly by the customer's account representative.
